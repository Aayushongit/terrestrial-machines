Architecting intelligence for any physical machine.

Terrestrial Machines is a research group studying how machines learn to move, balance, and manipulate the physical world. We train control policies in large-scale physics simulation and study their transfer to real hardware — across dexterous hands, legged systems, and whole-body humanoids.

Research Area 01

Dexterous manipulation

Many-fingered hands learn the fine motor skills needed to grasp and reorient objects using contact alone — a prerequisite for robots that can use human tools and environments.

Research Area 02

Legged locomotion

From four legs to many, our agents learn gaits that stay balanced, energy-efficient, and resilient — discovered from scratch through trial and reward.

Research Area 03

Whole-body humanoid control

High-degree-of-freedom humanoids are among the hardest systems to control. Our policies coordinate dozens of joints for walking, running, and dynamic motion.

In Development

Custom humanoid platforms

Our in-house humanoid models and mechanisms, under active research — from concept design and simulation through to fast, dynamic, and highly agile motion.
